# Final Four Weekend in a Sports City: How Las Vegas Handles Being Everyone's Home Court

Las Vegas has done something that no other American city has managed in quite the same way: it has become the neutral site venue for the entire country's sports emotions. When something big happens in sports — a championship, a major event, a marquee fight — Las Vegas is where the nation's sports energy converges.

Final Four weekend is a perfect example of what this means, what makes it extraordinary, and what it tells us about the city Las Vegas is becoming.

## The Universal Home Court Advantage

Every fan base that arrives in Las Vegas for Final Four weekend believes, with some justification, that this is their home court. Duke fans have been here before. Kansas fans have been here before. The Gonzaga faithful make a pilgrimage. Mid-major Cinderella schools bring the most passionate small traveling parties you've ever seen.

What they find: a city that doesn't belong to any of them — and therefore, somehow, belongs to all of them. The sportsbooks don't care who you root for. The casinos don't care. The restaurants are full of every fan base simultaneously, mixing in ways that in any other context would be logistically impossible.

This is the Las Vegas superpower in sports: the city is legitimately neutral. No home crowd advantage for anyone. Pure sport, played in a context where money changes hands on every possession.

## What Permanent Sports Teams Changed

The arrival of the Golden Knights, Raiders, Aces, and now Athletics has changed how Las Vegas relates to these neutral-site events. The city now has its own sports identity — its own teams, its own rooting interests, its own sports culture that persists year-round rather than only appearing when events arrive.

This creates something interesting during events like the Final Four: Las Vegas residents now experience the influx of visiting fans from the perspective of a sports city hosting an event, not just a tourist city capitalizing on sports. The person at the Circa sportsbook who's been coming in to watch the Raiders all season is now watching Final Four games alongside temporary visitors who are experiencing the same screen for the first time.

The local fans have become locals.

## The Crossover Sports Fan

Las Vegas has created a new type of American sports fan: the multi-sport polyglot who follows football in the fall, hockey in the winter and spring, baseball from spring through summer, and basketball year-round, who bets selectively on all of it, and who experiences sports as an integrated part of city life rather than a seasonal obsession.

This fan didn't exist at scale anywhere else in the country. Las Vegas created them by accident — by putting four professional sports teams in a city that was already processing enormous sports betting volume and hosting major events every month of the year.

The result is fans who know more, engage more broadly, and are more difficult to satisfy with mediocrity than the typical single-sport fan in a one-team market. They've raised the standards for what a fan experience should look like.

## Final Four as Preview

Final Four weekend in Las Vegas is, among other things, a preview of what permanent baseball looks like. The A's are watching how the city handles this kind of major event — the logistics, the fan behavior, the media coverage, the economic impact. The successful integration of the Final Four into the Las Vegas sports ecosystem validates the city's capacity to handle big events as a sports city, not just a convention city.

For A's fans: pay attention to how the city mobilizes around this weekend. The same infrastructure — the partnerships between venues, the transportation systems, the hospitality culture — is what the permanent A's stadium will leverage when the team is established and playoff games are coming.

Las Vegas isn't just good at hosting events. It's learning how to be a sports city while hosting them. The two things are feeding each other, and the result is something that didn't exist five years ago.

## The Fan's Weekend

If you're an A's fan living in Las Vegas this Final Four weekend: enjoy it. Absorb the energy. Watch some basketball. Bet something small. Experience what your city becomes when the national sports world descends on it.

And know that in a few years, when the A's make a playoff run and Las Vegas hosts post-season baseball for the first time, this is what that will feel like — except it'll be your team in the spotlight.

That day is coming. The fan base that was here first will have earned it.
